FIRST Robotics Competition Team 1277 Team Website

FRC is a competition between High Schools across the world to build a industrial sized robot to compete in a competition. Teams are given 6 weeks to design, build, and program a robot to accomplish a certain challenge given certain rules and specifications. These challenges involve using the robot to manipulate objects in the playing field to score points, and the year that I competed in involved climbing onto an elevated platform to score extra points.

    During my time as a member of the team, I:
  • Led a team of peers to develop software for the robot
    • Autonomous control of robot subsystems
    • Control of robot using direct user input
    • Monitoring of robot systems to prevent failures such as brownouts
    • Computer vision using a Raspberry Pi as a co-proccesor
  • Used Gradle to build, test, and deploy software
  • Worked with electrical and hardware teams to integrate sensors and motor control
  • Runner-up in both competitions in 2019
  • Competed in the New England Regional Championship in 2019
